TeleSound-Codec: High-Fidelity General Audio Coding Under 1kbps via Language-Grounded Supervision and Scalable Generative Modeling

Anonymous Authors

Abstract

High-fidelity general audio coding at ultra-low bitrates is crucial for applications ranging from low-bandwidth communication to generative audio-language modeling. However, existing neural codecs struggle at ultra-low bitrates, where weak semantic alignment causes semantic distortion under aggressive compression, and limited generative capacity leads to severe acoustic degradation and poor perceptual quality. To address these challenges, we propose TeleSound-Codec, a scalable general audio codec that preserves semantic integrity and fine-grained acoustic details together at ultra-low bitrates through language-grounded supervision and scalable generative modeling. Specifically, to realize strong semantic alignment, we propose language-grounded supervision, which trains a semantic encoder under the guidance of a pre-trained large language model (LLM), grounding codec latents in human-interpretable linguistic spaces. To improve the generative capacity, we further propose the scalable generative modeling via a flow-matching based generative decoder jointly learned with an acoustic encoder, allowing reconstruction quality to scale with computation under constrained bitrates. Experiments demonstrate that, when scaled to 1.8B parameters, TeleSound-Codec achieves high-fidelity reconstruction of 32kHz general audio—including speech, music, and other sound types—at bitrates as low as 0.275kbps.
